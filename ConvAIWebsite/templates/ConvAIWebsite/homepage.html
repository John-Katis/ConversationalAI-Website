Scope of the Project

Argumentation is both a key competence and an important cultural technique in democratic societies and is not only an essential part of our daily communication and thinking but also contributes significantly to the competencies of communication, collaboration and problem solving. Advances in intelligent agents using natural language processing (NLP) and artificial intelligence (AI) make the provision of dialogue-based argumentation learning frameworks possible. This can be leveraged to develop education agents for adaptive argumentation skill learning for use in both educational and business contexts.

Research Objectives

This study endeavours to combine natural language processing (NLP) frameworks in existence to develop novel tools and frameworks for argumentation learning. Educational agents are yet an emerging field of NLP and AI - one filled with unexplored potential and new horizons.The scientific aim of the project is to develop a novel comprehensive approach for automatic assessment of argumentative text quality and provision of adaptive, static feedback. A proof-of-concept for an argumentative dialogue agent with the ability to debate with users and a first prototype of such a framework are also within this project's aims.

Learning Design

The research project draws on the extensive network of academic partners of the Data Science and Natural Language Processing chair of the University of St. Gallen at the Institute for Computer Science (ICS) to maximize the impact of the research conducted. The project will be a collaboration with the aforementioned chair at the ICS, the Institute for Business Education and the Institute for Educational Management and Educational Technologies.
